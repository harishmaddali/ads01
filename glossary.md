# Digital marketing glossary

Short definitions of concepts you will see often in digital marketing. Use this as a reference while you learn channels, analytics, and campaign work.

## A

**A/B testing** — Running two (or more) versions of something—such as a headline, image, or landing page—to see which performs better, using consistent traffic and fair comparison rules.

**Ad network** — A platform that connects advertisers who want to show ads with publishers or apps that have space to show them (for example, display or video inventory).

**Attribution** — The rules used to assign credit for a conversion to one or more touchpoints (ads, emails, organic search, etc.). Models include last-click, first-click, linear, and data-driven.

**Audience** — The group of people you intend to reach, defined by demographics, interests, behaviors, or your own data (such as website visitors or customer lists).

## B

**Bounce rate** — The share of sessions where someone left without a meaningful second interaction. Interpretation depends on page type; a high rate on a blog post can be normal.

**Brand awareness** — How familiar people are with your brand. Often measured with reach, impressions, surveys, or branded search volume over time.

## C

**Call to action (CTA)** — The explicit next step you ask the user to take (for example, “Sign up,” “Book a demo,” “Shop now”).

**Click-through rate (CTR)** — Clicks divided by impressions (or eligible views), expressed as a percentage. Useful for comparing creative or placement strength.

**Content marketing** — Creating and distributing useful content (articles, video, guides) to attract and retain an audience, often supporting SEO and trust.

**Conversion** — A completed action you care about: purchase, lead form submit, signup, download, etc.

**Conversion rate** — Conversions divided by relevant visits or clicks, depending on how you define the funnel.

**Cost per acquisition (CPA) / cost per conversion** — Average cost to get one conversion. Often computed as spend divided by conversions in a period.

**Cost per click (CPC)** — What you pay (on average) for one ad click in auction-based channels.

**Cost per mille (CPM)** — Cost per thousand impressions. Common in display and awareness campaigns.

**Customer journey** — The path from first awareness through consideration to purchase and retention, including channels and messages at each stage.

## D

**Demand generation** — Activities that create interest and pipeline (often B2B), combining awareness, content, events, and paid programs.

## E

**Email marketing** — Sending messages to a permission-based list: newsletters, promotions, onboarding sequences, and transactional mail.

**Engagement** — Interactions such as likes, comments, shares, saves, or time on page. Definitions vary by platform.

## F

**Funnel** — A simplified model of stages (for example, awareness → consideration → conversion) used to plan messaging and measure drop-off.

## G

**Google Analytics (GA4)** — A common web analytics product for measuring traffic, events, and conversions on sites and apps.

## I

**Impression** — One display of your ad or one load of your content in a feed, depending on the channel’s definition.

**Inbound marketing** — Earning attention through content, SEO, and helpful experiences so people come to you rather than only pushing outbound messages.

## K

**Keyword** — A word or phrase people type into search engines or that platforms use to match ads to intent.

**KPI (key performance indicator)** — A metric you agree matters for a goal (for example, CPA, ROAS, leads, or qualified pipeline).

## L

**Landing page** — A focused page built for a single campaign or offer, designed to convert traffic from ads or email.

**Lead** — A person who has shown interest and shared contact information or equivalent signal, depending on your definition.

**LTV (lifetime value)** — The revenue or profit you expect from a customer over the relationship. Often compared to CPA for sustainable growth.

## M

**Marketing automation** — Software that runs triggered emails, lead scoring, and multi-step workflows based on behavior or CRM data.

**Martech (marketing technology)** — The tools stack: analytics, CRM, email, ads, CDP, etc.

## O

**Organic** — Traffic or reach you do not pay for directly at the moment of the visit (for example, unpaid social reach or SEO), though it still has creation and maintenance cost.

## P

**Paid media** — Advertising spend on platforms (search, social, display, etc.) to buy impressions, clicks, or conversions.

**Persona** — A semi-fictional profile of an ideal customer segment, used to align messaging and offers.

**Programmatic advertising** — Buying ad impressions through automated auctions and targeting, often across many sites or apps.

## R

**Remarketing / retargeting** — Showing ads to people who already visited your site or engaged with your brand, to bring them back.

**ROAS (return on ad spend)** — Revenue attributed to ads divided by ad spend. A simple efficiency metric for ecommerce-style goals.

**ROI (return on investment)** — A broader measure of gain versus cost; in marketing, be explicit about what you count as “return” and over what period.

## S

**SEM (search engine marketing)** — Often used to mean paid search; sometimes used broadly to include SEO and paid search together.

**SEO (search engine optimization)** — Improving visibility in organic search results through technical health, content, and authority signals.

**SERP (search engine results page)** — The page of results after a search, including ads, organic listings, and features like maps or snippets.

**Social proof** — Signals that others trust you: reviews, testimonials, user counts, logos, or UGC.

## T

**Tag / pixel** — A small piece of code that records events (page views, purchases) for analytics or ad platforms.

**Targeting** — Choosing who sees your ads or content using demographics, interests, geography, keywords, or first-party data.

## U

**UTM parameters** — Tags added to URLs (`utm_source`, `utm_medium`, etc.) so analytics can attribute traffic to campaigns and channels consistently.

**User-generated content (UGC)** — Content created by customers or fans (reviews, posts, videos), often repurposed in ads or on-site.

## V

**View-through conversion** — A conversion counted after someone saw an ad but did not necessarily click, within a platform-defined attribution window.

---

*This glossary is a starting point. Add channel-specific terms (for example, Quality Score in search ads) as you deepen work in each area.*
